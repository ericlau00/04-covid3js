Public Media

Displays patterns and analysis of public opinion during the COVID-19 pandemic

This line chart shows the amount of COVID-19 articles written over time. There is a dip in the number of articles written on the weekends because most people read news on business days. Thus, less articles will be written on the days where less people read them. We see that growth in COVID-related articles began on January 19, 2020. And February 23rd onwards, the number of articles began growing exponentially. This is around the time when the first case of local transmission of the virus was discovered in the United States (February 26, 2020). The number of articles start decreasing from March 29th onwards and start stagnating from April 5th onwards. We are not sure what causes this stagnation, perhaps because our data source did not discover any more articles in their time of recording this data.

This bar chart shows the subjectivity ratings of COVID-19-related news outlets (or domains). The subjectivity values range from 0.0 to 1.0, inclusive, where 0.0 is most objective and 1.0 is most subjective. This data shows that the most objective COVID-news sources are Wired, Finsmes, and Altassets. While the most subjective sources are Mashable, Iotworldtoday, and Dzone.

Commonly used words are larger and slightly faded in color. Less common words are smaller and darker.

The most common words are names of countries, days of the week, and the name of news outlets. The days of the week are to be expected because they are listed as part of the article publish date, among other details. The frequency in the names of proper locations (i.e. U.S., China, U.K.) shows how widespread COVID is, with China taking the lead in frequency (to be expected since Wuhan, China is COVID's location of origin).
